Pakistan's Independence Journey

1857

The War of Independence marks the beginning of the struggle against British colonial rule in the Indian subcontinent.

1906

The All-India Muslim League is founded in Dhaka to represent the interests of Muslims in British India.

1940

The Lahore Resolution is passed on 23rd March, formally demanding a separate nation for Muslims, which later becomes Pakistan.

1947

Pakistan gains independence from British rule on 14 August. Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah becomes the first Governor-General of Pakistan.

1948

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah passes away on 11 September, leaving a legacy of leadership and vision for the new nation.

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

Founder of Pakistan

Biography

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah (1876-1948) was the founder of Pakistan and its first Governor-General. His vision, leadership, and determination led to the creation of a separate homeland for Muslims of the subcontinent.

Born in Karachi, Jinnah studied law in London and became a prominent leader of the All-India Muslim League. He united Muslims under the demand for Pakistan and played a key role in the independence movement.

Jinnah's principles of unity, faith, and discipline continue to inspire the nation. He is remembered as Baba-e-Qaum (Father of the Nation).

Quaid-e-Azam passed away on September 11, 1948, but his legacy lives on in the hearts of all Pakistanis.

14 August

August 14 is celebrated in Pakistan with great enthusiasm as Independence Day. This day commemorates the creation of Pakistan in 1947, when under the leadership of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the dream of a free and sovereign nation became a reality.

It is a day that symbolizes freedom, unity, faith, and the countless sacrifices made to achieve an independent homeland.
